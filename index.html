I am a Postdoctoral researcher in the Distributed and Interactive Systems (DIS) group of CWI Amsterdam, since Aug 2019. I am hosted by Prof. Pablo Cesar. Prior to this, I did my Ph.D. from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering of IIT Kharagpur, India. My PhD supervisors were Prof. Bivas Mitra and Prof. Niloy Ganguly. During PhD, I was a member of the Complex Network Research Group (CNeRG). Before joining PhD, I have worked at several IT organizations (TCS, IBM, Capgemini) for more than 10 years.

My research interests lie in the areas of Affective Computing and Human-Computer Interaction. I work on different aspects of affective computing, ranging from emotion elicitation, data collection (physiological signals and self-report labels), to emotion model construction. My research has two broad themes: (a) to facilitate user engagement (e.g. survey fatigue and interruption management, better interaction approach) by addressing through a wide range of HCI methods (design, prototyping, user studies) (b) to customize and deploy state-of-the-art AI models in the context of various affective computing problems.
